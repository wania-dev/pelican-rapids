Editor's note

Wania Faisal

Dear Readers,

The incessant question, "When is the magazine coming out?" has been following me around like a determined shadow ever since mid-October. And quite rightly so, since it was supposed to come out in October. But we miscalculated (greatly) the challenges of making a new tradition. Setting a new trend. Revamping the initial concept for something fresh.

Think of this process like raising a child—it demands attention at the most unexpected times. Picture this: laboring through piles of submissions, trying to shape the magazine's vibe late into the night, and lots and lots of coding. Then picking its themes, assigning tasks, troubleshooting, and finally the magazine is born.

Truth be told, the magazine isn't just pixels on a screen; it's where PAFians have their moment. So, here it is, folks—the product of my labor, our Pelican Rapids Magazine. Enjoy the read, and let's hope our little mag grows up to be a beautiful reflection of the PAF Chapter student culture!

Cheers,
Wania

PAF Chapter Club Guide

Compiled by PR Staff

Introducing the PAF Chapter Clubs and Societies scene, where passion meets action, and students explore their talents, carving their unique paths. The PR staff has compiled this FAQ Guide to help you navigate the clubbing scene (definitely not the party one). Whether you're curious about joining, managing, or simply exploring multiple societies, this FAQ guide is your key to reaping the full benefits college societies have to offer.

Q: How do I join a club at PAF Chapter A-levels?
Clubs at PAF Chapter don't require formal memberships. Stay connected on WhatsApp groups or Instagram for activity updates, and you're in!

Do I need to attend regular club meetings?
Most clubs don't have regular meetings. Activities are usually announced, and you can join as your schedule permits. JPSS is the only club that holds bi-weekly debate training sessions. Led by the PAF Chapter debate coach, Sir Nayel, these engaging bi-weekly parliamentary debate sessions consist of personalized one-on-one training, mostly unfolding during the Tuesday and Thursday breaks from 11:20 to 12:10.

How can I get involved in club management?
Connect with club organizers, express your interest, and you might find yourself contributing to the administrative side of the club.

What benefits come with joining a club?
Joining a club expands your social circle, provides a great time, and shows your active campus involvement—essential for future uni applications.

How do club activities enhance my student experience?
Clubs help you pursue passions like writing, debating, charity work, or photography. They're a platform to discover and use your talents.

How often do clubs meet?
Clubs usually don't have regular meetings, or meet at all for that matter. They mostly post activity notices on WhatsApp or Instagram. Some, like JPSS, have bi-weekly sessions on Tuesdays and Thursdays for parliamentary debate training.

Can I join multiple clubs?
Absolutely! There's no limit. Join as many clubs as you're interested in and make the most of your time at PAF Chapter.

How can I contribute ideas to club activities?
Stay active in club communication channels. When activity announcements come up, share your ideas, and watch your suggestions turn into exciting events!

What if I'm unsure which club to join?
Attend different clubs' activities, see what interests you, and connect with organizers. Your ideal club might surprise you!

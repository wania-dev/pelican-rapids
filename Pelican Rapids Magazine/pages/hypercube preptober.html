What is Hypercube

Just Entertainment or a Catalyst for Academic Evolution?

Hypercube, the biggest science Olympiad in Karachi, is a grand event hosted by The City School PAF Chapter. This event blends an impressive array of modules spanning across all fields of knowledge, designed to engage students from diverse academic backgrounds. But is Hypercube merely a source of entertainment, or does it play a more profound role in shaping the academic landscape?

At Hypercube, you'll find a variety of STEM and general modules. From fun challenges like Block Buster and Criminal Investigations to educational pursuits like Pharma Lure and Neural Nexus, there's something for everyone. Teams from various schools and colleges come together under one roof, putting their skills and knowledge to the test, demanding critical analysis and strategic deployment of intellectual prowess.

Yet, Hypercube is more than just a contest. It's a platform where participants can unleash their creativity, connect with people from diverse backgrounds, and broaden their horizons. It's an opportunity to embark on a journey of learning and personal growth.

Hypercube's popularity continues to soar, with an ever-increasing number of participants from different schools and colleges each year. The event has expanded significantly and garnered widespread appreciation. The 5th edition of Hypercube was a game changer, hosting around 1,000 participants. However, Hypercube VI took this legacy to new heights, accommodating a whopping 1,700 participants.

The anticipation and excitement surrounding each Hypercube edition continue to grow. The City School PAF Chapter always strives to introduce something new. This year, they've rolled out exciting new modules like Hustlers Playground, Cyber Craft, Celestial Exodus, Outbreak, Verdict, and Blockbuster Breakout.

So, is Hypercube just a source of entertainment or a catalyst for academic evolution? Dive into this captivating journey and discover the answer for yourself!

By MARIUM AZIZ

The Special Track

Is different always good?

This year Karachi's biggest Science Olympiad marked its 7th iteration - with a nontraditional approach. A shift in strategy caused the splitting of the event into two distinct sections - Special Track and Main Track, diverging from the typical five-day flagship event managed entirely by the A-level section.

The special track held on November 10th and 11th at PAF Chapter set the stage for this novel approach. While the special track involved a few unprecedented hurdles and internal drama within the student body in the pre-event phase, however, these hiccups only emphasize the adage: no success without failures.

Post-event analysis is currently underway in order to fine tune the imminent Main Track set for the following weekend.

Notably, the student body's ability to orchestrate an event of this magnitude despite these challenges is a testament to the adaptive and safe learning environment the PAF Chapter faculty provides. Even though the event has evolved in great ways, its core objects remain centered on celebrating, appreciating, and spotlighting STEM fields.

by FRANZ KAFKA

One morning, when Gregor Samsa woke from troubled dreams, he found himself transformed in his bed into a horrible vermin. He lay on his armour-like back, and if he lifted his head a little he could see his brown belly, slightly domed and divided by arches into stiff sections. The bedding was hardly able to cover it and seemed ready to slide off any moment.

His many legs, pitifully thin compared with the size of the rest of him, waved about helplessly as he looked. "What's happened to me?" he thought. It wasn't a dream. His room, a proper human room although a little too small, lay peacefully between its four familiar walls. A collection of textile samples lay spread out on the table - Samsa was a travelling salesman - and above it there hung a picture that he had recently cut out of an illustrated magazine and housed in a nice, gilded frame. It showed a lady fitted out with a fur hat and fur boa who sat upright, raising a heavy fur muff that covered the whole of her lower arm towards the viewer.
